Positions

A brokerage account holds assets in the form of cash and securities.

The total amount of cash held in the account is called its Cash Position.

Securities are held in groups of shares called lots (or Lot Positions). A lot consists of shares of the same security purchased or acquired in a single transaction. For example, a lot of 500 shares of AAPL purchased on 12/01/2010. Lots have a purchase price (price paid per share) associated with them. We use the term Instrument Position to denote the total quantity of a security held in an account. For example, an account holding two lots of AAPL containing 100 and 200 shares respectively, is said to have a position of 300 shares in AAPL.

The collection of all assets in an account, including its cash and lot positions, is called the Account Position.
